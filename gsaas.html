GSAAS

Global Ground Infrastructure.Operationally Unified

Kepler Aerospace operates a globally distributed Ground Station as a Service (GSaaS) platform engineered for reliable satellite command, control, and data downlink across multiple orbital regimes.

Kepler's Unified Command Center (KUCC) integrates multi-band ground antennas, virtualized baseband systems, and automated scheduling into a single operational architecture. The platform is designed to support both single-satellite missions and constellation-scale deployments without increasing operational complexity.

KUCC allows operators to focus on payload and mission objectives while we manage the ground infrastructure layer.

Global Network Architecture

The network is engineered to provide predictable pass performance, operational redundancy, and scalable throughput.

Mid- and high-latitude deployment mix

Distributed routing architecture to reduce single-point dependency

Designed for Modern Satellite Operations

As constellations expand, manual coordination and station-by-station integration become limiting factors.

KUCC incorporates:

  • API-based scheduling interfaces
  • Software-defined modem environments
  • Automated pass-by-pass configuration
  • Centralized remote monitoring
  • Machine-to-machine integration capability

This architecture enables operators to manage fleet operations through a unified interface rather than individual ground assets.
